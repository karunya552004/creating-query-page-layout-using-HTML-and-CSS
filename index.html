Frequently Asked Questions

Why is the moon sometimes out during the day?

The moon reflects sunlight, and it can be seen during the day when the sun is shining on it.

Why is the sky blue?

The sky appears blue because of a phenomenon called Rayleigh scattering. This occurs when sunlight is scattered by tiny molecules in the Earth's atmosphere.

How do airplanes stay up?

Airplanes use aerodynamic lift to stay up. Their wings are designed to create a difference in air pressure, which generates an upward force.
